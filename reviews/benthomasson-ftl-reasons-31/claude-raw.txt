## Overall Assessment

**The change addresses the core issue** — three independent copy-pasted rebuild implementations are consolidated into one canonical `_rebuild_dependents()` method on Network. This directly fixes the "fragile denormalization" problem for the rebuild path.

**What remains unaddressed:**

1. **Incremental mutation paths are still fragile.** The methods `add_node` (line 112-118), `add_justification` (line 406-411), `supersede` (line 374), `challenge` (line 484), and `convert_to_premise` (lines 568-574) all do manual incremental `dependents.add()`/`dependents.discard()` updates. These are the exact paths the issue warns about. If any of them gets the logic wrong, the index silently drifts. The `_rebuild_dependents` docstring claims "All other rebuild call sites should delegate here" but these incremental sites don't rebuild — they do targeted adds/discards that could diverge from the canonical logic.

2. **`verify_dependents()` is test-only.** The issue suggested an integrity check after mutations. The method exists but is never called in production. Adding an `assert not self.verify_dependents()` in debug builds or after key mutations would fully close the gap.

3. **Performance regression in `_update_node_justifications`** — full rebuild per call in a loop. Not a correctness issue but worth noting.
